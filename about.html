About ATURE

ATURE was founded by Linh Dinh, a visionary entrepreneur with deep roots in Vietnamese culture. Growing up in the Ha Tinh province of central Vietnam, Linh was part of what she calls the "lonely generation"—children whose parents left home to seek better opportunities abroad. Her father moved to Korea shortly after her birth, and her mother worked in the government, leaving Linh to spend her days observing the world around her, particularly the farm near her home.

As she watched the farmers in her community, Linh noticed a troubling trend: fewer and fewer were returning to the fields. Many, like her parents, had left for greener pastures, either abroad or in other industries. This exodus left a void in the rural communities and a disconnect from the rich cultural heritage that these lands held.

In 2013, the Vietnamese government launched the "One Commune, One Product" (OCOP) program, aimed at revitalizing local economies by focusing on the unique products and crafts of each commune. This initiative sparked Linh's imagination. She saw an opportunity to not only preserve but also celebrate the authenticity of Vietnamese culture through its products.

Years later, Linh found herself in the United States, studying at Northeastern University for a master's degree in project management. It was here that she recognized another gap: while the U.S. is a significant importer of Vietnamese goods, there was a lack of authentic, culturally rich products available to consumers, especially within the Vietnamese diaspora.

This realization led to the birth of ATURE—a platform designed to connect small and medium-sized producers in rural Vietnam with consumers in the U.S. ATURE aims to bridge the gap between the high-quality, culturally significant goods produced in Vietnam and the demand for authentic Vietnamese products in the American market.

Linh's vision for ATURE is deeply personal. "I do everything based on my own childhood, and based on the love I have for the kids in Vietnam," she says. "I don’t want any kid to grow up without parents, like I did." By creating economic opportunities for families in Vietnam, ATURE seeks to enable them to thrive at home, preserving the cultural fabric of their communities.

Today, ATURE is more than just a marketplace; it's a storybook of Vietnamese heritage, where each product and shared memory tells a tale of tradition, craftsmanship, and community. Through ATURE, Linh invites you to explore, connect, and be part of a movement that celebrates and sustains Vietnamese culture.

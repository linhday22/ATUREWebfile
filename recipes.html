Flavors of Memory

What does your home taste like? From phở broth to bánh mì—these recipes hold lineage.

Phở Bò

A traditional beef noodle soup, simmered with star anise and cinnamon, carrying the warmth of a Hanoi morning. My uncle taught me to skim the broth just as the first light touched the Mekong, a ritual as sacred as the meal itself...

Cultural Note: Phở is more than a dish—it’s a story of family, resilience, and the rhythm of Vietnam’s mornings.

Bánh Mì

A fusion of French baguette and Vietnamese flavors—pâté, pickled carrots, and a hint of fish sauce—a story of cultural harmony. I remember my mother slicing the bread on our small wooden table in Da Nang, her smile as bright as the morning sun...

Cultural Note: Bánh mì reflects Vietnam’s history of adaptation, blending colonial influences with local ingenuity.

View Recipe

Chè Ba Màu

A vibrant dessert of three colors, blending mung bean, coconut milk, and pandan jelly, a sweet memory of Saigon street vendors. My sister and I would race to the corner stall, coins clutched in our hands, the taste of chè forever tied to those carefree days...

Cultural Note: Chè ba màu is a celebration of color and community, often shared during festive gatherings.
